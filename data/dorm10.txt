Lived there for all my time at MSU so I know the building quite well. Cafeteria is great but insanely crowded at lunch. Either eat lunch before 11:30 or after 1:30. At dinner, there's a big rush when it first opens at 5pm, but then it becomes much more normal.

Shaw does have a great location. It it right across from the CATA station, so if you have any farther classes, getting a bus pass is worth your while. Same if you're parking your car in lot 89. Walking to Grand River isn't too bad.

Computer lab is decent and so is the gym (though the gym used to be waaaaay better when they had free weights, now it's just cardio).

Laundry facilities are decent. If you do laundry in the morning (really, any time before 10am) you'll have all the machines to yourself. Afternoon and evening laundry can be crowded.

I highly recommend keeping your door open if you want to be social. But also, go into other people's open doors just to say hi and introduce yourself. Even ask if anyone wants to grab lunch. You make a lot of friends that way if you put yourself out there just a little bit. You won't get that many takers, but people will remember you and say hi to you.

Lastly, Shaw is community bathrooms. Learn when your floor usually gets cleaned every day and work that into your schedule. (Our floor usually got cleaned somewhere between 9:30 and 10am every day, which is right when some people would be getting ready for their 10:20 class.) I know it seems awkward but (at least by the sinks) that's another place to be social on the floor.

One more tip is to give your RA ideas for what the floor might want to do for bonding (euchre tournament, smash bros, etc.). Also tell them to have food at the event. Every floor RA has access to some funds that they can put on events with and some money can be put towards buying food. We had a few events in out floor with Buffalo Wild Wings and Insomnia Cookies.
